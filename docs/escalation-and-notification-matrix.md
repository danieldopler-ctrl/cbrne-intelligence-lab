# Escalation And Notification Matrix

This matrix supports review and documentation. It is not legal guidance and does not replace an organization's reporting duties or emergency procedures.

| Level | Condition | Internal route | External posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| `TL0` | Relevant record only | Log with provenance | None |
| `TL1` | Weak indicator or observation requiring monitoring | Analyst disposition | None based solely on automation |
| `TL2` | Material rule trigger or repeated pattern | Senior analyst / duty lead review | Assess only if new actionable information or duty exists |
| `TL3` | Corroborated or significant consequence signal requiring urgent review | Lead plus designated security/operations/reporting authority | Authorized referral assessment |
| `TL4` | Immediate danger or mandatory-report condition | Emergency leadership; preserve evidence after response begins | Do not delay `911`, local responders, or required reporting |

## External Routing Reference

| Verified circumstance | Possible authorized path |
|---|---|
| Immediate danger | `911` or local emergency responders |
| Suspicious chemical, biological, or radiological threat activity | FBI WMD reporting channel or local FBI office |
| Reportable environmental discharge | National Response Center (`800-424-8802`) |
| Applicable explosive material theft or loss from stock | ATF (`800-461-8841`) and appropriate local authorities |
| Suspected intentional biological or toxic event | Public-health authorities plus FBI/JTTF/WMD coordination as appropriate |

## System Controls

- No outbound phone call, email, tip, or regulatory report is automated.
- `TL3` and `TL4` cases require a recorded notification assessment before closure.
- The record must capture the decision maker, date/time, route, rationale, reference number when provided, and follow-up owner.
